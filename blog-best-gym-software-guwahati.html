Best Gym Management Software in Guwahati & Assam (2026 Guide)

If you run a gym in Guwahati, Assam, or anywhere in Northeast India, you've probably tried managing members with a register, an Excel sheet, or a WhatsApp group — and watched renewals slip through the cracks. This guide explains what to look for in gym management software built for how gyms here actually run, and how to pick one without overpaying.

Why "global" gym software doesn't fit gyms in Assam

Most popular gym software is built for the US or Europe. They assume members pay by credit card, get reminders by email, and book classes through an app. In Guwahati and the rest of the Northeast, the reality is different:

  • Members pay by UPI or cash — not cards.
  • Everyone lives on WhatsApp — email reminders get ignored.
  • Owners want something simple and cheap, not a ₹5,000/month enterprise suite.

So the best gym software for an Assam gym is the one that speaks UPI and WhatsApp natively — not one you have to bend to fit.

A checklist: what to look for

1. WhatsApp renewal reminders (automatic)

The single biggest revenue leak in any gym is members who simply forget to renew. Software that automatically sends a WhatsApp reminder a week before expiry — and again on the day — pays for itself many times over.

2. UPI payments, no gateway hassle

Members should be able to pay you directly by UPI (GPay, PhonePe, Paytm) — money straight to your account, no card machine, no per-transaction fees eating your margin.

3. Dead-simple member management

You should see at a glance who's active, who's expiring soon, and who's lapsed. Adding a member should take seconds, and importing your existing register (even a messy Excel sheet) shouldn't be a fight.

4. Works on a phone

Your receptionist runs the front desk from a phone. The software has to work well on mobile, not just a laptop.

5. Honest pricing

For a single-location gym, you shouldn't be paying more than a few hundred rupees a month. Look for a real free trial so you can test before you commit.
